SCIENCE FICTION AND CRIME

COWBOY BEBOP, ODD TAXI, MICHIKO AND HATCHIN

Even outside the sporting context, Brazil also features prominently in science fiction and crime anime. These types of works often include elements primarily related to music and the anime's setting. This is the case in works like Cowboy Bebop and Michiko and Hatchin, which combine Brazilian references to create different universes that blend fiction, social reality, and cultural influence.

COWBOY BEBOP (Action, Drama, Science Fiction, Space Western)

Author: Shinichiro Watanabe;

Manga release: 1997;

Anime release: April 1998;

Description: Cowboy Bebop follows a disorganized crew of bounty hunters traveling aboard the spaceship Bebop as they chase criminals while dealing with their own tragic pasts and existential traumas. The series is accompanied by an unforgettable jazz and blues soundtrack that reflects its themes of loneliness and melancholy.

Reference (s): Antônio Carlos Brasileiro de Almeida Jobim.

Throughout the anime

Throughout the series, three elderly men appear in several episodes. Their names are Antônio, Carlos, and Jobim—an explicit homage to Antônio Carlos Brasileiro de Almeida Jobim, one of the most important figures in Brazilian music and the father of Bossa Nova..

ODD TAXI (Comedy, Crime, Drama, Mistery)

Author: Kazuya Konomoto;

Manga release: January 2021;

Anime release: June 2021;

Description: Hitoshi Odokawa is an ordinary walrus taxi driver. His routine has become so monotonous that it may be affecting his mental health. He begins experiencing visions that seem real, and as he seeks medical help, his world starts to collapse. Problems surround him and eventually enter his taxi.

Reference (s): capoeira.

Episodes 4 and 10

During episode 10, the character demonstrates her capoeira skills by defending her taxi driver friend. This shows that although capoeira may seem like a type of Brazilian dance, it is also a form of self-defense.

MICHIKO E HATCHIN (Action, Adventure, Comedy, Crime Fiction)

Author: Takashi Ujita;

Manga release: No manga adaptation;

Anime release: Outober 2008;

Description: Michiko & Hatchin follows the fugitive criminal Michiko Malandro and the young orphan Hana Morenos (Hatchin) as they escape together across a country called Diamandra, inspired by Brazil and Latin America. In search of Hatchin’s father, the series explores themes of freedom, family, and harsh social realities, supported by a vibrant soundtrack.

Reference (s): Brazilian-inspired setting, pastel, and bossa nova.

Throughout the anime

Throughout the anime Michiko and Hatchin, we are introduced to several Brazilian references. Some of these include the setting (although the anime takes place in a fictional country, it is said to have been inspired by Brazil), the pastries, and the soundtrack.

In addition to its visual elements, Michiko & Hatchin stands out for its soundtrack, which was largely composed by Brazilian musician Kassin, with contributions from artists such as BNegão and Thalma de Freitas. This collaboration creates a unique atmosphere for the series.
